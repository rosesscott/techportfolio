In the Photoshop unit...
This year in photoshop I created my own Julie Opie inspired selfie and my own Beth Hoeckel inspired portrait.
These are the images I inspired my Beth Hoeckel protrait off...
I chose these images with the concept of making my landscape look supernatural. I wanted to incorporate the idea of a cave with an image of the moon, to give off the idea that someone is 'stranded' on a cave near space.
This is my finished Beth Hoeckel portrait.
I created this portrait using various techniques in Photoshop, including the eraser tool, move tool, transform tool and lasso tool. As you can see, I erased the original background in the cave and added my own moon and star pictures. I then added in a picture of a sillouhette to make it seem like a human is stranded on some type of supernatural cave near the moon but near water also. Lastly I adjusted the colouring (such as the exposure and saturation) of the image to match it with it's surroundings.
This is my finished Julian Opie inspired selfie.
I created this portrait using various techniques in Photoshop, including the brush tool, paint bucket tool and picker tool. I started by outlining my original picture with the brush tool to form the base of my selfie. I then added more dimension with different thickness of lines and various colours.
